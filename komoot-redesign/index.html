Komoot Redesign

Reshaping Komoot's product experience end to end — from the first onboarding tap to the last saved route — with a focus on clarity, speed of iteration, and measurable growth.

Year
2024
Role
Product Designer
Duration
12 weeks
Tools
Figma, FigJam, Notion

The challenge

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit. The app had accumulated years of surface-level patches on top of a visual language that no longer fit where the product was headed. Conversion from trial to paid had plateaued, and the onboarding flow was doing more explaining than guiding.

The brief was simple on paper: find the 20% of changes that would move the biggest numbers, and ship them without a rebrand.

Approach

We ran a two-week audit across the highest-traffic surfaces, shadowed a dozen user sessions, and mapped every friction point against the app's growth funnel. The outcome was a small, tightly-scoped backlog: a rebuilt onboarding, a clearer paywall, and a map screen that finally read as the product's hero.

Design decisions lived close to engineering — a shared Figma + code tokens library kept the handover boring in the right way, which meant iteration could happen in days, not sprints.

Outcome

The redesigned flow shipped in three increments over a quarter and moved the metrics we wanted: a lift in onboarding completion, a meaningfully better trial-to-paid conversion, and clearer data on where the next iteration should go next.

Beyond the numbers, the bigger win was a shared vocabulary between design and engineering that survived the project — and a codebase that made it easier to keep moving after the launch.
