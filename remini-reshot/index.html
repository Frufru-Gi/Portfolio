Remini Reshot

A new AI-driven feature inside Remini that lets users "re-shoot" their photos — different angle, different light, same subject. Built for consumer-grade delight and near-instant feel.

Year
2024
Role
Product Designer
Duration
6 weeks
Tools
Figma, Principle, Notion

The challenge

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet. AI photo features are crowded — everyone has a filter, everyone has an upscale. Reshot needed to feel like a power move: a single tap that changes the photo meaningfully, without asking the user to learn prompting or read copy.

The other constraint was latency. Anything over ~4 seconds felt like "work"; anything under felt like magic.

Approach

We designed the interaction around a single, confident primary action — "Reshot" — and kept every configurable choice out of the first screen. Advanced controls live one layer deeper, for the 10% of users that want them.

Perceived-performance design carried a lot of the weight. A layered reveal and a subtle shimmer made the ~3s generation feel purposeful instead of empty.

Outcome

Reshot shipped as a headline feature of the app's quarterly release. It became one of the most-used AI features within two weeks, and became a case study for the team on how to package a single AI moment into a clear, no-prompt product.
